Alaskan Huskey Facts

#1 Unlike Siberian Huskies, Alaskan Huskies tend to be much calmer.


While these dogs do require large amounts of exercise to keep their mental stability, they tend to be very calm in their down time (at least compared to the Siberian cousins).

#2 Alaskan Huskies are very intelligent but also quite stubborn


These dogs are considered highly intelligent and have the capability to be well trained but missing out on rigorous early training programs can lead them to be very independent and stubborn in their later years.

#3 They were bred to be sled dogs


Alaskan Huskies are a working breed and their soul purpose is to pull sleds in a team.

#4 Alaskan Husky is not technically a bred


Alaskan Huskies are a crossbreed of several other breeds that are well suited to sled pulling. As such these dogs can look slightly different and even have different types and colors of coats.

#5 They are considered a very fast dog


On a full sled dog team, this breed can average around 8 miles per hour. The most famous dog sled race, the Iditarod, is 1,100 miles long and a team of dogs can complete it just over a week. Some Alaskan Huskies have even been clocked at 28 miles per hour!
