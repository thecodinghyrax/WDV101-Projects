Why are Labradors so great?

Labs Are Highly Intelligent

Due to their high intelligence as a breed, people in need can benefit from Labradors’ services in different fields. They are well known as ideal guide dogs for the disabled or the blind. When appropriately trained, they can also make excellent guard dogs. They can hear something as faint as a pin drop from a distance. Also, Labs have an exceptional sniffing ability. Their services are also famous for recovering bodies after a natural disaster, finding a missing person, and detecting various drugs and explosives.

They Have Wonderful Temperaments

Labradors are handy, friendly, and well-balanced dogs, adaptable to a wide variety of functions as well as making good pets. Labs are not overly prone to moping, hypersensitivity, insecurity, or aggression. They have an excellent reputation for being an even-tempered breed and a perfect family dog.

Labradors Are Easy to Train

Every dog needs training if it’s to become a member of the family. Certain dog breeds are hard to train, but Labradors require less energy and effort to train than most. They are quick learners and quite intelligent. Therefore, proper and complete training requires less time, and if you are hiring a dog trainer, it will save on your pocketbook. Even first-time dog owners find it easy to train a Labrador. Known for being very food-driven dogs, you will find out how well behaved your Lab can be when you have a tasty treat in your hand.

Labradors Are Loving Dogs

If you look into a Lab’s eyes, you will see that it looks at you with pure love. When she sits close to you and looks at you, you can tell that she is thinking about how much she loves you. When Labs are tired, hurt, or scared, and you're there with them, you'll see how thankful they are to have you nearby. They are also delighted to see their owner when you come home.

Labs Are Great With Children

Do you want to tire your kids out? Then you should get them a toy that never tires, a Labrador. Kids love labs, and labs love kids. A Labrador will allow itself to be poked, prodded, and hugged, and still give a lot more love back. They are always ready to chase you around for hours and still want more.
